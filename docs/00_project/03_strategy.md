# Strategy Brief

## Market Opportunity

The global data centre construction market was valued at $275 billion in 2025 and is projected to reach $761 billion by 2034 (CAGR ~12%). The urgency for better site selection tooling has never been higher — $64 billion in US data centre projects have already been blocked or delayed by local opposition, and 72% of developers rate power and grid capacity as a "very or extremely challenging" siting factor. Water demand at AI data centres is projected to rise 170% by 2030, making resource-aware site selection a mission-critical capability right now.

## Target Customer Profile

**Persona**: Marcus, 42, VP of Development at a mid-market data centre developer
**Role**: Responsible for identifying and validating new build sites across Europe and North America
**Daily frustration**: His team spends 3–6 months on preliminary due diligence for sites that ultimately fail — killed by grid interconnection queues, water stress, or local council opposition. By the time the risk is visible, millions in planning costs and 12+ months of runway have been lost. He needs to kill bad sites in days, not quarters.

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | What they do | Their gap |
|---|---|---|
| DatacenterHawk | Real estate market intelligence platform — tracks existing colocation supply, hyperscale leasing, and transactions | Covers existing inventory only; no water, grid, or community risk scoring for greenfield sites |
| Site Selection Group | Consulting-led advisory covering power, real estate, tax incentives, and economic development for large clients | Human-delivered reports — slow, expensive, not scalable; no real-time risk monitoring |
| GE Vernova Consulting | Engineering-led site selection with deep grid interconnection modelling and GIS-based environmental mapping | Narrowly power-focused; no water or community/political risk; consulting model, not a platform |

## Your Differentiator

Terrascope is the only platform that combines water resource availability, energy grid capacity, and community/political risk into a single AI-native scoring layer — turning months of bespoke consulting into a self-serve screening tool that any developer can run across an entire site portfolio in hours. The unfair advantage: no incumbent offers all three risk dimensions in one product, and none are built to scale with AI.

## Landing Page Strategy

**Hero message**: Speak directly to the pain — project cancellations and wasted capital. The hero should say something like: *"Know before you build. Terrascope surfaces water, energy, and community risks before you commit a single dollar to a site."* High-stakes, data-forward, authoritative.

**3 feature cards should highlight**:
1. **Water & Cooling Risk** — Hydrological stress scores, watershed availability, and cooling constraint flags for any candidate site
2. **Energy Grid Intelligence** — Grid capacity, interconnection queue status, and renewable access — the #1 constraint killing projects today
3. **Community & Political Risk** — Local opposition sentiment, planning ordinance exposure, and moratorium risk signals before you engage planners

**CTA**: *"Join the waitlist"* — positioned as early access to a platform reshaping how data centre real estate gets done. Tie it to urgency: developers who screen smarter move faster than their competitors.
